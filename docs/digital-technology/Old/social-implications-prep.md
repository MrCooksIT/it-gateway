# Social Implications — Grade 8 & 9 Revision

## Key concepts to know

- **Responsible and ethical use** of technology
- **Cyberbullying** — what it is and how to respond
- **Digital citizenship** — rights and responsibilities online
- **Copyright and plagiarism** — using others' work correctly
- **Privacy and personal information** — what to share and what not to
- The **digital divide** — why not everyone has equal access to technology
- **E-waste** — impact of discarding technology

## Quick definitions

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Cyberbullying | Using technology to bully, harass, or intimidate someone |
| Digital citizen | A person who uses technology responsibly and ethically |
| Copyright | Legal protection for someone's original creative work |
| Plagiarism | Presenting someone else's work as your own |
| Piracy | Illegally copying or distributing copyrighted software, music, or film |
| Digital divide | The gap between people who have access to technology and those who don't |
| E-waste | Discarded electronic devices that can harm the environment |
| Phishing | Tricking people into giving personal information through fake messages |

## Practice questions

1. Define cyberbullying and give **two** examples of cyberbullying behaviour.
2. A learner copies text from a website and submits it as their own work. What is this called? Why is it wrong?
3. Explain what the digital divide is. Give **one** reason why it exists.
4. List **three** types of personal information you should NOT share online.
5. What is the difference between copyright and piracy?
6. A friend shares a pirated movie with you. Is it legal to watch it? Explain.
7. Explain **two** environmental impacts of e-waste.
8. Describe **two** ways to practise good digital citizenship.

## Typical test format

Your test on this topic will likely include:
- Definitions of key terms
- Scenario questions (what should the learner do?)
- True/False with justification
- Short paragraph responses on ethics or impact
