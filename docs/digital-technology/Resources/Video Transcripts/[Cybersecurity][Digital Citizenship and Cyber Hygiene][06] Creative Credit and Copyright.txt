Copyright is a form of legal protection for intellectual property, usually applying to artistic works. Any work of original thought is the legal property of the creator, and this is protected by copyright law. So the owner or creator of the work has the right to decide how their work is used or distributed.

There are several types of copyright licenses, some that offer more protectoin than others.

Copyright Licenses
All rights reserved

Some rights reserved

Public domain


There are a lot of different types of licenses, but they all fit into one of these three categories. All rights reserved, some rights reserved, or public domain.

Copyright Licenses
All rights reserved
Creator reserves all rights
Some rights reserved
Creator may allow for things like reuse or modifications as long as they are cited as the creator
Public domain
Creator has dedicated the work to the public domain and waived all rights


All rights reserved means that the creator reserves all rights, and the work can’t be distributed, reused or modified without their explicit permission. This is the most strict copyright license. Some rights reserved means that the creator might allow for some things like reuse or modifications, as long as they are cited as the creator. The specific license spells out exactly how other people might be allowed to use their work.
Finally, there is public domain, which means the creator has dedicated the work to the public domain and waived all rights. This means we are free to reuse or modify the work as we wish.
