# Digital Citizenship & Cyber Hygiene — Grade 9

## Unit Overview

In Grade 8, you were introduced to the foundations of digital citizenship: what a digital footprint is, why cyberbullying is harmful, how to stay safe online, and how to think critically about information you find on the internet. You built a solid foundation.

Grade 9 goes further. This unit treats you as a more capable, more critical thinker — one who is ready to engage with the legal frameworks, technical concepts, and ethical dilemmas that shape life in the digital world. Where Grade 8 asked "what is this?", Grade 9 asks "why does this work this way, what are the consequences, and what should we do about it?"

:::info From Foundation to Depth
Grade 8 introduced the **concepts**. Grade 9 examines the **implications**. You will encounter the same eight topics as last year, but each one is explored with greater technical detail, stronger legal grounding, and more demanding critical thinking.
:::

This unit draws on the **CodeHS Digital Citizenship & Cyber Hygiene** framework and is aligned with the South African Grade 9 Digital Technology curriculum. Throughout, you will engage with South African legislation, real-world case studies, and scenarios that require analysis — not just recall.

## Unit Topics

---

### 1. Digital Footprint & Reputation

**File:** [digital-footprint.md](./digital-footprint.md)

At Grade 9 level, managing your digital footprint is about more than just thinking before you post. It is about understanding the commercial ecosystem that profits from your data — including data brokers who compile detailed profiles about you without your knowledge. You will explore the concept of **personal branding**, understand South Africa's **Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA/POPI Act)**, and learn about the **right to be forgotten**. You will also learn how to conduct a professional social media audit.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain how data brokers operate and how they use your personal information
- Apply the concept of personal branding to manage a professional digital identity
- Describe the key provisions of the POPI Act relevant to individuals
- Evaluate your own social media presence and propose improvements
- Analyse how companies profile users based on digital footprint data

---

### 2. Cyberbullying

**File:** [cyberbullying.md](./cyberbullying.md)

Grade 9 moves from recognising cyberbullying to understanding its legal consequences under the **Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020**, its psychological impact according to research, and the systemic approaches organisations and governments use to address it. You will analyse policies, evaluate platform moderation practices, and engage with restorative justice as an alternative to punitive responses.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify specific offences and penalties under the Cybercrimes Act 2020
- Analyse the psychological research on cyberbullying's impact
- Design an anti-cyberbullying policy for a school or online community
- Compare punitive and restorative justice approaches to cyberbullying
- Evaluate how social media platforms moderate harmful content

---

### 3. Internet Safety

**File:** [internet-safety.md](./internet-safety.md)

Basic phishing awareness is upgraded to a full understanding of **advanced social engineering attacks**: spear phishing (targeted), smishing (SMS), and vishing (voice calls). You will learn about **VPNs** — what they do and what they do not do — and how to stay safe on public networks. South African reporting mechanisms, including the **SAPS Cybercrime Unit**, are covered in detail.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Distinguish between phishing, spear phishing, smishing, and vishing
- Explain how social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technology
- Describe what a VPN does and its limitations
- Identify risks specific to public Wi-Fi networks
- Explain how to report cybercrime in South Africa

---

### 4. Privacy & Security

**File:** [privacy-security.md](./privacy-security.md)

Building on passwords and 2FA from Grade 8, this chapter tackles the legal architecture of privacy in South Africa. The **POPI Act** is examined in depth — what it requires from organisations, what rights it gives you, and what happens when it is violated. Technical topics include **encryption**, **HTTPS and TLS**, how data breaches occur, and what secure data deletion means.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain the key provisions of the POPI Act and your rights as a data subject
- Describe how encryption protects data in transit and at rest
- Explain how HTTPS and TLS work in practical terms
- Analyse how data breaches occur and what organisations must do in response
- Implement device security and multi-factor authentication practices

---

### 5. Information Literacy

**File:** [information-literacy.md](./information-literacy.md)

Grade 9 information literacy focuses on the most sophisticated information threats: **deepfakes**, **astroturfing**, and **propaganda techniques** designed to manufacture consent and manipulate behaviour. You will learn the **lateral reading technique** used by professional fact-checkers, understand how **search algorithms and SEO** shape what information reaches you, and distinguish between academic and popular sources.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Identify deepfakes and explain the technology used to create them
- Explain what astroturfing is and how to recognise it
- Apply lateral reading to verify sources independently
- Explain how search engine algorithms and SEO affect the information you find
- Use APA or Harvard citation style to reference digital sources correctly

---

### 6. Creative Credit & Copyright

**File:** [creative-credit-copyright.md](./creative-credit-copyright.md)

South Africa's **Copyright Act 98 of 1978** (and its proposed amendments) is examined in the context of the digital age. You will explore the distinction between **moral rights** and **economic rights**, understand how **derivative works** and **transformative use** work legally, analyse controversies around **music sampling**, and get a working knowledge of **open source software licences** (GPL, MIT, Apache). **Digital Rights Management (DRM)** and the **DMCA** are also covered.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Distinguish between moral rights and economic rights in copyright law
- Analyse whether a given use of copyrighted material constitutes fair use or infringement
- Compare open source software licences and their conditions
- Explain what DRM is and evaluate arguments for and against it
- Describe how DMCA takedown notices work

---

### 7. Hacking Ethics

**File:** [hacking-ethics.md](./hacking-ethics.md)

Grade 9 examines cybersecurity as a career path and a discipline. You will learn about **penetration testing methodology**, **responsible disclosure**, and the growing demand for ethical hackers. The **Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020** is examined section by section for the specific offences and penalties it defines. Broader ethical questions — including **state-sponsored hacking**, the **ethics of surveillance**, and **whistleblowing** — are explored for the critical thinkers in the room.

**Learning Outcomes:**
- Describe the methodology of a penetration test
- Explain what responsible disclosure is and why it matters
- Identify cybersecurity career paths and certifications (CompTIA Security+, CEH)
- Reference specific sections of the Cybercrimes Act 2020 and their penalties
- Evaluate the ethics of state surveillance and whistleblowing

## How This Unit Is Assessed

Digital citizenship is assessed through a combination of:

- **Knowledge questions** — recall and explanation of key terms and concepts
- **Application questions** — applying concepts to realistic scenarios
- **Analysis questions** — examining case studies, policies, and arguments
- **Evaluation questions** — making and defending a reasoned judgement about an ethical or legal question

The **Check Your Understanding** section at the end of each chapter includes all four types. In assessments, look for the **cognitive demand** of each question — "define" and "list" questions are lower order; "analyse," "evaluate," and "justify" are higher order.

## South African Legal Framework

Several South African laws are discussed throughout this unit. Here is a quick reference:

| Law | What It Covers | Relevant Chapter(s) |
|---|---|---|
| **Cybercrimes Act 19 of 2020** | Cyberbullying, hacking, data interception, malware | 2, 4, 7 |
| **Protection of Personal Information Act (POPI Act / POPIA)** | How organisations collect, store, and use personal data | 1, 4 |
| **Copyright Act 98 of 1978** | Intellectual property rights in creative works | 6 |
| **Films and Publications Act** | Harmful online content, age restrictions | 3 |
| **Electronic Communications and Transactions Act (ECTA) 2002** | E-commerce, electronic contracts, cybercrime (predecessor to Cybercrimes Act) | 4, 7 |

:::warning A Note on the Law
South African digital law is evolving rapidly. The Cybercrimes Act only came into full effect in 2021. The Copyright Amendment Bill has been a subject of ongoing debate. Always verify current law with up-to-date sources. The descriptions in this unit reflect the law as it stood in 2025.
:::

## Why Grade 9 Digital Citizenship Matters

You are 14 or 15 years old. Within two to four years, you will be applying to universities and bursaries. Within five to seven years, you will be entering the workforce or higher education. The decisions you make online right now are building a permanent record.

But beyond personal consequences, the digital world is increasingly where society's most important debates happen — about truth, about rights, about power and accountability. Understanding digital citizenship at a deeper level makes you a better-informed citizen, not just a safer internet user.

:::tip Think About This
Every piece of technology discussed in this unit was built by people. People decided how it would work, what it would prioritise, and who would benefit. Understanding how these systems work is the first step to being able to shape them — not just be shaped by them.
:::
