# Environment & Society

Technology powers the modern world — but at a cost. The devices we use daily consume enormous energy, create hazardous waste, and contribute to carbon emissions. At the same time, ICT offers powerful tools for addressing environmental challenges. Understanding both sides is essential for responsible digital citizenship.

> [!NOTE] Grade 12
> Environmental impact of ICT, distributed computing, and AI/robotics in society are primarily Grade 12 topics.

## Environmental Impact of ICT

### E-waste — Electronic Waste

**E-waste** is discarded electronic equipment — old phones, computers, TVs, batteries, and other electronics.

**Why e-waste is a serious problem:**
- Electronics contain **toxic materials**: lead, mercury, cadmium, beryllium, arsenic
- These leach into soil and groundwater at landfill sites
- Informal e-waste "recycling" in developing countries exposes workers to toxins
- E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream globally
- SA generates over 350,000 tonnes of e-waste per year

| Toxic material | Found in | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead** | Solder, CRT screens | Damages nervous system and kidneys |
| **Mercury** | LCD backlights, batteries | Damages brain and nervous system |
| **Cadmium** | Rechargeable batteries | Causes cancer, kidney damage |
| **Beryllium** | Connectors | Causes lung disease |
| **Flame retardants** | Circuit boards | Hormone disruptors |

### Responsible E-waste Management:
- **Recycle** through certified e-waste collection points
- **Donate** working devices instead of discarding
- **Buy refurbished** — extend device lifespan
- **Manufacturer take-back** — some brands accept old devices
- **Certified recyclers** — Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE)

### Energy Consumption

ICT uses enormous amounts of electricity:

| Source | Consumption |
|---|---|
| Data centres | ~1–2% of global electricity |
| Networks (routers, towers) | ~1% of global electricity |
| User devices | ~2% of global electricity |
| **Total ICT** | ~4–5% of global electricity |

**Data centres:**
- Google, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft operate hundreds of data centres globally
- A large data centre uses as much electricity as a small city
- Require constant cooling — additional energy cost
- Google has committed to 100% renewable energy

**Bitcoin and cryptocurrency:**
- Proof-of-work mining extremely energy intensive
- Bitcoin network uses ~120 TWh/year — more than Argentina

### Green ICT Solutions:
- **Renewable energy** — solar/wind powered data centres
- **Energy-efficient hardware** — ARM processors, efficient SSDs
- **Virtualisation** — fewer physical servers needed
- **Cloud computing** — better utilisation of shared infrastructure
- **Power management** — sleep modes, automatic shutdown

### Carbon Footprint of ICT

| Device | Estimated CO₂ for manufacturing |
|---|---|
| Smartphone | ~70 kg CO₂ |
| Laptop | ~300–400 kg CO₂ |
| Desktop PC | ~400–600 kg CO₂ |
| Server | ~1–2 tonnes CO₂ |

**Key insight:** Most of a device's carbon footprint comes from **manufacturing**, not use. Keeping devices longer significantly reduces your carbon impact.

**Actions that help:**
- Keep devices longer before replacing
- Buy refurbished/second-hand devices
- Choose energy-efficient models
- Recycle properly

## ICT and Society

### AI and Automation

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is the ability of computer systems to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence.

| AI Application | Industry | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Medical diagnosis | Healthcare | AI detects cancer in images with >90% accuracy |
| Customer service | Retail/banking | Chatbots handle routine queries |
| Fraud detection | Finance | Real-time transaction analysis |
| Content recommendation | Entertainment | Netflix, Spotify, YouTube algorithms |
| Autonomous vehicles | Transport | Self-driving cars, trucks |
| Language translation | Communication | Google Translate |
| Writing assistance | Many | AI-generated text (ChatGPT) |

**Positive impacts of AI:**
- Improves medical diagnosis accuracy
- Reduces human error in dangerous or repetitive tasks
- Makes personalised services possible at scale
- Solves complex optimisation problems (logistics, energy)

**Negative impacts of AI:**
- Job displacement — routine tasks automated
- Algorithmic bias — AI can discriminate
- Deepfakes — AI-generated misinformation
- Privacy concerns — AI requires vast data
- Autonomous weapons — ethical issues around AI in warfare

---

### Robotics

**Robots** are programmable machines capable of carrying out complex actions automatically.

| Sector | Use | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Manufacturing** | Assembly lines, welding, painting | Replaced many factory workers |
| **Logistics** | Warehouse picking, delivery drones | Amazon, DHL use robots |
| **Healthcare** | Surgical robots, pharmacy robots | More precise surgery |
| **Agriculture** | Automated harvesting, planting | Addresses labour shortage |
| **Mining** | Autonomous vehicles, drilling | Reduces danger to workers |
| **Domestic** | Robot vacuum cleaners | Household convenience |

**Advantages of robots:**
- Work 24/7 without fatigue
- No human error in repetitive tasks
- Safe in hazardous environments
- More consistent quality

**Disadvantages:**
- High capital cost
- Job displacement — fewer factory/manual jobs
- Cannot handle unpredictable situations (yet)
- Maintenance costs

---

### Distributed Computing

**Distributed computing** uses multiple computers networked together to solve a large computing problem — each handles a portion of the work.

**Examples:**
- **BOINC / SETI@home** — volunteer computers helping analyse radio telescope data
- **Folding@home** — protein folding simulations for disease research
- **Blockchain** — distributed ledger across thousands of computers
- **Grid computing** — research institutions sharing computing power

**Benefits:**
- Harnesses unused computing power of millions of devices
- Solves problems too large for any single computer
- Cost-effective — uses existing volunteer hardware
- Resilient — no single point of failure

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **E-waste** | Discarded electronic equipment |
| **Carbon footprint** | Total greenhouse gas emissions from an activity |
| **Green ICT** | Environmentally responsible use of technology |
| **AI** | Artificial Intelligence — computer systems performing tasks requiring human intelligence |
| **Robotics** | Programming and use of machines to perform complex actions |
| **Distributed computing** | Multiple networked computers solving one large problem |
| **Automation** | Using technology to perform tasks previously done by humans |
| **Blockchain** | Distributed ledger across many computers |
| **Deepfake** | AI-generated fake video or image of a real person |
| **Algorithmic bias** | AI producing unfair results due to biased training data |
