# Online Safety

Every time you connect to the internet, you enter a space with both opportunity and risk. Understanding how online threats work — and what you can do to protect yourself — is not optional in the modern world.

> [!NOTE] Grade 10–11
> Online safety (email threats, safe internet use, digital footprint) is introduced at Grade 10. Grade 11 extends with more detailed threat types and protection measures.

---

## The Online Threat Landscape

| Category | Threats |
|---|---|
| **Privacy threats** | Data collection, surveillance, digital footprint |
| **Social engineering** | Phishing, smishing, vishing, pretexting |
| **Technical threats** | Malware, ransomware, hacking, DDoS |
| **Identity threats** | Identity theft, account takeover |
| **Content threats** | Cyberbullying, misinformation, inappropriate content |
| **Financial threats** | Scams, fake e-commerce, card fraud |

## Phishing and Social Engineering

**Social engineering** manipulates people into revealing information or taking actions that compromise security — exploiting human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

### Types of Phishing

| Type | Channel | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Phishing** | Mass email | Anyone |
| **Spear phishing** | Targeted email | Specific person/organisation |
| **Whaling** | Targeted email | Senior executives (CEO, CFO) |
| **Smishing** | SMS | Mobile users |
| **Vishing** | Phone call | Anyone |
| **Pharming** | Redirect website | Anyone visiting a compromised site |

### How to Identify Phishing Emails

| Red flag | Example |
|---|---|
| Urgent language | "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!" |
| Generic greeting | "Dear Customer" instead of your name |
| Sender mismatch | Display name says "FNB Bank" but email is `fnb-help@gmail.com` |
| Suspicious link | Hover shows `http://fnb-scam.ru/login` |
| Grammar errors | Poor spelling, awkward phrasing |
| Requests credentials | "Reply with your password to confirm" |
| Unexpected attachments | Unsolicited invoice or report |

## Safe Internet Practices

### Passwords
- Use **long, unique passwords** for every account (16+ characters)
- Use a **password manager** to generate and store passwords
- Never reuse passwords across sites
- Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** on every important account
- Never share passwords — no legitimate service will ask for your password

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
A second verification step beyond just your password:

| 2FA Method | Description |
|---|---|
| **SMS OTP** | One-time password sent to your phone |
| **Authenticator app** | Time-based code (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator) |
| **Hardware key** | Physical key plugged into USB (YubiKey) |
| **Biometric** | Fingerprint or face recognition as second factor |

### Safe Browsing
- Check for **HTTPS** and padlock before entering any personal data
- Be suspicious of sites with many pop-ups or redirect you unexpectedly
- Don't click on links from unknown emails or SMS
- Use **private/incognito mode** for sensitive browsing on shared computers
- Clear browser cache and cookies on shared/public computers

### Public Wi-Fi
- **Avoid accessing sensitive accounts** (banking, email) on public Wi-Fi
- Use a **VPN** to encrypt traffic on public networks
- Disable auto-connect to open networks
- Verify the network name with the venue (fake hotspots)

## Digital Footprint

Your **digital footprint** is the trail of data left by everything you do online.

### Passive digital footprint
Data collected without your active input:
- IP address and location
- Websites visited (cookies, server logs)
- Device type, browser, operating system
- Search queries
- Purchase history

### Active digital footprint
Data you deliberately create:
- Social media posts
- Comments and reviews
- Forms filled in
- Photos uploaded
- Emails sent

### Why it matters:
- Employers search candidates' online presence before hiring
- University admission offices check social media
- Insurance companies may use data for risk assessment
- Marketers build profiles for targeted advertising
- Law enforcement can access digital records

### Managing your digital footprint:
- Set social media profiles to **private**
- Think before posting — public posts can last forever
- Search your own name to see what's visible
- Delete old accounts you no longer use
- Use privacy settings on every platform
- Be careful what permissions you grant apps

## Cyberbullying

**Cyberbullying** is the use of technology to repeatedly harass, threaten, or humiliate someone.

| Form | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Harassment** | Sending threatening or abusive messages |
| **Exclusion** | Deliberately leaving someone out of online groups |
| **Impersonation** | Creating fake accounts pretending to be the victim |
| **Outing** | Sharing private information or images without consent |
| **Trolling** | Posting inflammatory content to provoke a reaction |
| **Cyberstalking** | Following and monitoring someone's online activity |

### Effects on victims:
- Anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem
- Social withdrawal
- Academic performance drops
- In severe cases: self-harm or suicide

### What to do:
- **Don't respond** — responding often escalates
- **Block** the person on all platforms
- **Screenshot** evidence before blocking
- **Report** to platform (every major platform has reporting tools)
- **Tell a trusted adult** — parent, teacher, or counsellor
- **Report to SAPS** if threats are made

## Online Scams

| Scam Type | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Advance fee fraud** | "You won a prize — pay a small fee to claim it" |
| **Romance scam** | Build online relationship, then request money |
| **Job scam** | Fake job offer requiring upfront payment |
| **Fake e-commerce** | Site that takes payment but never delivers |
| **Lottery scam** | Notification that you've won a lottery you never entered |
| **Tech support scam** | Caller claims to be from Microsoft; your computer has a virus |

### Rule of thumb:
> **If it sounds too good to be true, it is.**

## Safe Use of Social Media

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Set profiles to private | Share your address or daily routine |
| Think before posting | Post while angry or emotional |
| Verify information before sharing | Share unverified news or rumours |
| Use strong, unique passwords | Use your name or birthday as password |
| Review app permissions | Grant all permissions without checking |
| Be selective about friend requests | Accept requests from people you don't know |

## Screen Time and Digital Wellbeing

| Issue | Consideration |
|---|---|
| **Addiction** | Social media designed to maximise engagement → difficult to stop |
| **Blue light** | Screens before bed interfere with sleep |
| **Posture** | Prolonged screen use can cause neck and back problems |
| **Eye strain** | Follow 20-20-20 rule: every 20 min, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds |
| **Social comparison** | Social media shows highlights, not reality → unrealistic expectations |

## Exam Focus

> [!IMPORTANT] High-Frequency Questions
>
> 1. **"Give FOUR signs that an email might be a phishing attempt"** — urgent language; generic greeting; sender email doesn't match organisation; suspicious links; requests for passwords; grammar errors; unexpected attachments
>
> 2. **"What is a digital footprint? Give ONE example of a passive and ONE of an active footprint"** — trail of data left by online activity; passive: websites visited tracked by cookies; active: social media post you published
>
> 3. **"Give FOUR safe internet practices"** — use strong unique passwords; enable 2FA; check for HTTPS before entering data; avoid sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi; use VPN on public networks
>
> 4. **"What is cyberbullying? What should a victim do?"** — using technology to repeatedly harass/humiliate someone; victim should: not respond, block the bully, screenshot evidence, report to platform, tell a trusted adult
>
> 5. **"Why should you use a VPN on public Wi-Fi?"** — Public Wi-Fi is unencrypted — anyone on the network can intercept your data; VPN creates an encrypted tunnel protecting data from eavesdropping
